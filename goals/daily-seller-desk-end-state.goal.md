# Daily Seller Desk End State

## Objective

Lead Machine becomes the seller desk a salesperson opens every morning to know who to call, what happened last, and what to do next.

## End-State Experience

A seller can:

- log in and return to the same workspace
- set what they sell, where they sell, good-customer criteria, and disqualifiers
- search or refresh saved markets
- review verified and ranked leads
- see call-now, verify-first, no-answer, interested, follow-up, not-relevant, and archived queues
- call manually through normal browser/phone behavior
- log notes, outcomes, owner, and follow-up dates
- see activity history across calls and manually/synced email events
- export useful call lists and workspace snapshots
- use read-only or approval-based agent help without losing control

## Product Agent Boundary

Agents may enrich selected leads, flag missing contact data, summarize source evidence, suggest verification, prepare follow-up context, and propose queue/status changes for seller approval.

Agents must not sell for the user, call, email, scrape private profiles, generate ready-to-send outreach, or override seller judgment.

## Success Criteria

- Sellers can start the day from the command center and queues.
- Workspace state persists across sessions and users/teams according to the workspace model.
- Recommendations are traceable to source evidence and workflow history.
- Email-connected activity is attached to leads/workspaces without mass sending.
- The product remains useful without Proff, SSB, broad OSINT, or sales automation.
